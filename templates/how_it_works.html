üé≤ 10 Prediction Methods

Each method uses a different statistical approach to select numbers. When you request multiple predictions, each uses a different method:

1

Frequency Based
(Hot Numbers)

How it works: Selects the most frequently drawn numbers from recent draws (last 100 draws).

Theory: "Hot" numbers that appear often are more likely to appear again.

Best for: Players who believe in trending numbers.

2

Balanced
(Hot & Cold)

How it works: Mixes 3 frequently drawn numbers (hot) with 3 rarely drawn numbers (cold).

Theory: Balance between popular and overdue numbers.

Best for: Risk-balanced strategies.

3

Overdue Numbers

How it works: Selects numbers that haven't appeared in the longest time.

Theory: Numbers "due" for a comeback based on probability.

Best for: Contrarian players.

4

Pattern Based

How it works: Analyzes patterns like odd/even and high/low number distribution.

Theory: Lottery draws tend to have balanced distributions.

Best for: Pattern recognition enthusiasts.

5

Statistical Average

How it works: Selects numbers with frequency close to the statistical average.

Theory: Numbers near the mean are most "normal" and likely.

Best for: Data-driven players.

6

Recent Trends

How it works: Focuses only on the last 10 draws to find immediate trends.

Theory: Short-term patterns can indicate current lottery behavior.

Best for: Momentum-based strategies.

7

Number Pairs Analysis

How it works: Identifies numbers that frequently appear together in the same draw.

Theory: Certain number combinations have stronger correlation.

Best for: Combination-focused players.

8

Sum-Based Targeting

How it works: Generates numbers whose sum matches the historical average (¬±10%).

Theory: Winning combinations tend to have sums near the average.

Best for: Mathematical approach enthusiasts.

9

Odd/Even Balanced

How it works: Ensures exactly 3 odd and 3 even numbers.

Theory: Most lottery draws have balanced odd/even distribution.

Best for: Balance-focused strategies.

10

Spread Distribution

How it works: Selects numbers evenly spread across the entire 1-37 range.

Theory: Avoids number clustering for wider coverage.

Best for: Diversification strategies.
